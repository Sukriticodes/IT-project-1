About Sudoku

Sudoku is a popular logic-based number puzzle that challenges players to fill a grid with digits so that certain conditions are met. Although it uses numbers, Sudoku is not a math game — no calculations are needed. Instead, it requires reasoning, logic, and pattern recognition.Sudoku became widely popular in the early 2000s, though its origins trace back to number puzzles in 18th-century Europe and a modern form first published in Japan in the 1980s. Today, Sudoku appears in newspapers, apps, websites, and competitive puzzle events worldwide.

Why People Love Sudoku

                  It improves focus and logical thinking. It can be played casually or competitively. It offers different difficulty levels, from easy to expert.

Rules to play Sudoku

Sudoku is a logic-based puzzle game with simple but strict rules. Here's how to play:

Objective:

1.Fill a 9×9 grid with digits 1 to 9 so that:
2.Each row has every digit from 1 to 9 exactly once.
3.Each column has every digit from 1 to 9 exactly once.
4.Each 3×3 box (subgrid) has every digit from 1 to 9 exactly once.
Basic Rules:

1.No Repetition:
A number must not repeat in any row, column, or 3×3 box.

2.Use Only Digits 1–9:
You can only use digits from 1 to 9 (no letters, symbols, or zeros).

3.Fixed Numbers Can’t Be Changed:
The puzzle starts with some numbers already filled in. These are clues and cannot be erased or changed.

4.Use Logic, Not Guessing:
A well-designed Sudoku puzzle always has one solution that can be reached using pure logic—no guessing needed.

5.Work Through Possibilities:
It helps to look at rows, columns, and boxes to see which numbers are missing and what options are valid for each empty cell.
